# 06 — Sales channels

The product is its own best ad — a deployed page that *looks* like a deployed page costs less attention to evaluate than any ad copy. The channel mix exploits that.

## Channel 1 — Self-referential proof on HN, X, and design Twitter

**Why it fits**: Maya's circle reads HN comments and design X. The strongest demo is the URL bar showing `landing-pages-on-demand.prin7r.com` while the page narrates *"this site you're reading was generated in 27 minutes by the product itself."*

**Tactics**:
- Show HN post: "I built 20 landing pages in one afternoon by giving Claude a playbook — here are all 20." Links the prin7r-projects portfolio.
- A single X thread per week, posted Tuesday 10am ET: hero screenshot, brief input, deployed URL, time elapsed.
- Reply-guy budget: replying to "anyone know a good landing page service?" tweets with a 2-sentence mention + link, no formal ad spend.

**KPIs**: HN front page → 60 → 100% lift on signups for 24h; X thread → 8–15 trial briefs per send.

## Channel 2 — Niche Slack/Discord communities

**Why it fits**: Daniel buys after a peer says it works. The cheapest peer reach is unfilteredmention in 6 well-chosen rooms.

**Target rooms**: Demand Curve, RevGenius, Indie Hackers Slack, Reforge alumni, OnDeck founders, design-twitter-adjacent Discords (Layers, etc).

**Tactics**:
- Soft launch: 3 rooms, 1-paragraph intro + "free first brief if you mention this room."
- Co-marketing: free landings for the operators of these communities in exchange for a self-referential testimonial.

**KPIs**: 5–8 rooms × 2 briefs/room = ~12 briefs from a single round of seeding.

## Channel 3 — Founder/agency newsletter ecosystem

**Why it fits**: Daniel reads 4 marketing newsletters; Maya reads 2 founder newsletters. A single classified-style placement converts when the brand looks correct in the placement.

**Targets**: Demand Curve newsletter, MarketingDive, Workweek's *Brilliant Marketers*, founder newsletters (Lenny's, ofo).

**Tactics**:
- Sponsorship of one marketing newsletter at $1.5k–$3k for a single send, measured strictly by attributed signups.
- Avoid: paid "growth-hack" newsletters; the audience doesn't match.

**KPIs**: target $80 CAC blended across the first 3 newsletter sends.

## Channel 4 — Marketplace listings (Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, BetaList)

**Why it fits**: A launch day on PH gets the design crowd to evaluate the artifact directly.

**Tactics**:
- Time PH launch with the Show HN.
- Pre-load 30 hunters via a private notification list assembled from prior trial users.

**KPIs**: PH top 5 → 2,000–4,000 visits → 200–400 trial briefs in 48h.

## Channel 5 — SEO long tail (intent: "alternative to X")

**Why it fits**: "Webflow alternative for landings", "Durable AI alternative", "fast landing page service" — the buyer is in evaluation mode, not awareness mode.

**Tactics**:
- 12 comparison posts, each a side-by-side gallery of 4 alternative outputs vs Pagewright's output. Each post deploys a *real* Pagewright output of the same brief and links to it.
- The comparison posts themselves are deployed using Pagewright's own pipeline (more self-referential proof).

**KPIs**: 10–20 organic signups/mo per post once indexed (~3 months).

## Channel 6 — Done-for-you concierge (premium / inbound)

**Why it fits**: clinics, real-estate operators, and other non-technical buyers who can't fill a brief alone.

**Tactics**:
- "Talk to a human" CTA on the landing leads to Calendly.
- 20-min Zoom; concierge fills the brief on-screen; landing is live before the call ends.
- Priced at $1,200 + $99/mo retainer.

**KPIs**: 5 calls/wk × 40% close = 2 paid concierge customers per week.

## Channel mix (90-day, post-launch)

| Channel              | % of acquisition | Spend (mo 1) | Spend (mo 3)   |
|----------------------|------------------|--------------|----------------|
| Self-ref proof + organic social | 35%   | $0           | $0             |
| Niche communities    | 20%              | $0–$200      | $0–$400        |
| Newsletters          | 20%              | $3,000       | $4,500         |
| PH / IH launch       | 10%              | $0           | spent month 1  |
| SEO long-tail        | 10%              | $1,500 (writer) | $2,000      |
| Done-for-you inbound | 5%               | $0           | $500 (Calendly+Zoom seats) |

Total month-1 spend: ~$4,500. Target month-1 paying customers: 25 (self-serve) + 4 (concierge).
